Most Asked Question About PET

Why need to do vaccinate your pet?
You should vaccinate your pet to protect them from dangerous, potentially fatal infectious diseases like parvovirus, distemper, and rabies. Vaccinations also safeguard other animals, prevent the spread of diseases to humans (like leptospirosis), and are often required for travel and boarding. The benefits of vaccination, which stimulates the body to create disease-fighting antibodies, significantly outweigh the small risks of a minor injection reaction, like soreness or a small bump.
What should I do when cat bite me?
If a cat bites you, immediately wash the wound with soap and water, apply an antiseptic if available, and cover it with a clean bandage. Seek medical attention promptly, especially for deep bites or if you don't know the cat's vaccination status, as cat bites can cause serious infection. Keep the wound clean and monitor it for signs of infection like redness, swelling, warmth, or fever, and see a doctor again if these appear.
How to take care of your pet?
To care for a pet, provide essential daily care including nutritious food, fresh water, and a safe, clean living space. Offer regular exercise and mental stimulation, and ensure routine veterinary visits for vaccinations and health checks. Grooming, affection, and quality time are also crucial for a pet's overall physical and mental well-being.
